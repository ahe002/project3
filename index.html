ABOUT
font

Meaning: Kodchasan (Thai meaning - elephant)
Background: a part of the set of freely licensed computer fonts developed for Thai by the Thao government, nicknamed the "national fonts"
Designer: Cadson Demak, a font foundry, (remastered version for GoogleFonts), Kansuda Piamprachakphong (original font, only in Thai and English)
Debuted on September 7, 2010 (officially announced and released as a part of the National Fonts by the Council of Ministers). In 2018, the fonts were revised and released with the introduction of the Open Font License.

Available in: Afrikaans, Albanian, Catalan, Croatian, Czech, Danish, English, Estonian, Finnish, French, German, Italian, Latvian, Lithuanian, Maltese, Hungarian, Polish, Romanian, Slovak, Slovenian, Spanish, Norwegian, Swedish, Turkish, Vietnamese, Zulu
